# Digital Ambassador Report: UPI "Collect Request" Fraud Case Study

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is currently one of the most common financial cybercrimes in India. Unlike technical hacks that break into a system, this crime uses social engineering to trick people into giving away their own money. It relies on a lack of digital literacy rather than a software vulnerability.

## The Process: How the Scam Works
1. **Finding a Lead:** Scammers usually find targets on marketplace apps like OLX or through social media comments where students might be selling textbooks or gadgets.
2. **Building Trust:** They pose as a quick buyer or an official representative to lower the victim's guard.
3. **The Trap:** Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a "Collect Request" on an app like GPay or PhonePe. 
4. **The Misconception:** They tell the victim they need to scan a QR code or enter their UPI PIN to "receive" the payment.
5. **The Theft:** Once the victim enters their PIN, the money is instantly withdrawn from their account. Because a PIN is only for sending money, the victim has unknowingly authorized a theft.

## Impact and Takeaways
The targets are often students or young adults who are comfortable with tech but might not know the specific mechanics of a transaction. The consequences are tough because these transactions are instant and very hard to reverse. This highlights why it is so important for us to understand that receiving money never requires a PIN.
