# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

## Financial & UPI Safety
1. **UPI Safety Rule #1 (The Golden Rule):** Remember that entering your UPI PIN is *only* required for sending money, never for receiving it. If an app asks for your PIN to receive funds, it is a scam.
2. **Verify Before You Pay:** Always double-check the receiver's UPI ID and the registered name displayed on your app screen before authorizing any financial transaction.

## General Digital Safety
3. **Password Protection:** Never share your passwords, OTPs (One Time Passwords), or UPI PINs with anyone, including individuals claiming to be university staff or bank representatives.
4. **Enable 2FA:** Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for all your email, social media, and banking accounts to add an extra layer of security.
5. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking:** Avoid using public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks (like at cafes, airports, or campus common areas) for online banking or accessing sensitive personal information. Use your mobile data instead.
6. **Software Updates:** Keep your laptop's OS, smartphone apps, and antivirus software updated to patch known security vulnerabilities.
7. **Beware of Phishing:** Be highly skeptical of unsolicited emails, SMS messages, or WhatsApp texts containing urgent requests, unknown links, or suspicious attachments.
8. **Official Apps Only:** Download applications exclusively from official sources like the Google Play Store or Apple App Store to avoid downloading hidden malware.

## 🚨 How to Report a Cybercrime in India
If you or someone you know is a victim of a cybercrime, do not panic. Report it immediately using the official government channels:

* **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Cybercrime Helpline:** Call **1930** (Available 24x7)
