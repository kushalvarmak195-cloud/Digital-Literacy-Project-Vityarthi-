# Social Media Checklist for College Students

## 5 Do's

- [ ] Audit your privacy settings regularly to control who can view your personal posts and information.
- [ ] Maintain a polite, professional tone when interacting with university staff, professors, or tech recruiters online.
- [ ] Pause and think critically before posting—remember that digital footprints are nearly impossible to erase.
- [ ] Use platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub to positively network with peers and showcase your coding projects.
- [ ] Verify the credibility of news or articles before sharing them to avoid spreading misinformation.

## 5 Don'ts

- [ ] Post or share offensive, discriminatory, or highly controversial content.
- [ ] Share sensitive personal details like your home address, phone number, passwords, or banking information.
- [ ] Engage in public arguments, cyberbullying, or trolling in comment sections.
- [ ] Use inappropriate or overly casual profile pictures on platforms linked to your academic or professional identity.
- [ ] Plagiarize or post other people's intellectual property (including code snippets) without giving proper credit.
